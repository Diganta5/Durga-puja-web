About Durga Puja

We, the villagers of Biswas and Nunnan, come together every year to organize this grand Durga Puja. Being proud Bengalis, these five days hold a special place in our hearts. It’s a time when we unite, celebrate, and immerse ourselves fully in the festivities. With devotion, joy, and enthusiasm, we enjoy every moment — from worship and rituals to cultural performances, making this a memorable experience for everyone.

Puja Schedule

Mahapanchami (October 8, 2024)

The festival commences with the formal inauguration of Durga Puja. Preparations for the pandal and intricate decorations begin early in the day. By the evening, the sacred Durga Bodhon ceremony is performed, marking the invocation of Goddess Durga's divine presence.

Mahasashti (October 9, 2024)

The Sashti Puja takes place, accompanied by various community events. A blood donation camp is organized to contribute to society, followed by a vibrant cultural program. Outstanding students from Madhyamik and Higher Secondary exams are also felicitated for their achievements.

Mahasaptami (October 10, 2024)

The day begins with the ceremonial Nabapatrika Snan, a traditional bathing ritual performed amidst a grand royal procession. The Saptami Puja follows, invoking the blessings of the Goddess. The evening concludes with cultural performances that captivate the audience.

Mahashtami & Mahanavami (October 11, 2024)

Ashtami Puja, the most sacred day of Durga Puja, is marked by the offering of Puspanjali (flower offerings) and the powerful Sandhi Puja, which bridges Ashtami and Navami. Cultural programs in the evening add a festive spirit to the sacred celebrations.

Vijoyadashami (October 12, 2024)

On Dashami, the final day of Durga Puja, devotees gather for the immersion ceremony (Bisorjjon), bidding a tearful farewell to Maa Durga. Cultural performances follow to celebrate the last moments of the festival.

Kojagori Lakshmi Puja (October 16, 2024)

The festivities continue with the auspicious Kojagori Lakshmi Puja, dedicated to Goddess Lakshmi, the goddess of prosperity and wealth. Devotees offer prayers for abundance and blessings during this solemn occasion.

Social Activities

Blood Donation Camp

On the auspicious day of Mahasasthi (October 9, 2024), we proudly organize a Blood Donation Camp as a way to give back to the community. This life-saving initiative reflects the true spirit of Durga Puja — spreading kindness and supporting those in need.

You Are Cordially Invited!

Join us in this noble cause and help save lives. Your participation is valuable. Please visit our camp at 10 am, October 9, 2024, at the library.

Congratulating Outstanding Students

We take this opportunity to recognize and honor the academic achievements of exceptional students from Madhyamik and Higher Secondary exams. These young achievers will be congratulated on Mahasasthi, inspiring the future generation to continue striving for excellence.

The list of outstanding students will be updated soon. Please stay tuned for further updates!
